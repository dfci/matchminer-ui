What is MatchMiner?

MatchMiner is a computational platform for matching genomic profiles to open clinical trials at Dana-Farber Cancer Institute. It currently provides the following features:

  • Trial Search: For example, one can easily identify all trials requiring a BRAF or EGFR mutation, and further filter by cancer type. Trial search is open to all, and does not require a user name or password. Trial search is based on extensive genomic curation of precision medicine clinical trials currently active at {{ fc.ENV.resources.institution }}.

  • Patient-Specific Matching for Precision Medicine Clinical Trials: Oncologists can search for a patient and view genomically-matched clinical trials currently active at DFCI.

  • Patient Recruitment for Specific Clinical Trials: Clinical trial investigators can identify patients with a specific genomic alteration and get automatic email notifications of new patients who have received sequencing. Patient identification and notification is only available to approved clinical trial investigators. To apply for access, complete the online registration form..

  • MatchMiner also displays the results of molecular testing, including OncoPanel and ImmunoProfile reports.

How can I access MatchMiner?

MatchMiner may be accessed by navigating to https://matchminer.dfci.harvard.edu, or, through Epic Hyperspace.

To access MatchMiner in Epic when viewing a patients chart, go to the "More" menu at the bottom left of the screen (More > Rarely Used > PHS Applications > MatchMiner).

To access the MatchMiner Trial Search feature in Epic, go to the "Epic" menu at the top left of the screen (Epic > PHS Applications > DFCI Applications > MatchMiner Trial Search).

We encourage you to star access to MatchMiner, because this will give you easy access to MatchMiner from the Activity Menu in the left sidebar.

What data is in MatchMiner?

MatchMiner has data from OncoPanel, Rapid Heme Panel, and ImmunoProfile.

OncoPanel and Rapid Heme Panel data is generated by the Center for Advanced Molecular Diagnostics at BWH and is updated nightly.

ImmunoProfile data is generated by the ImmunoProfile team and is available in MatchMiner immediately upon signout.

All data is clinical or covered by Profile research consent (DFCI protocols 11-104 or 17-000).

What clinical trials are currently in MatchMiner?

MatchMiner includes precision medicine clinical trials currently active at {{ fc.ENV.resources.institution }}. This specifically includes any active clinical trial that lists a genomic biomarker, such as a gene mutation or copy number alteration, as required eligibility criteria. Because we manually curate each trial, we do not currently include or curate trials beyond {{ fc.ENV.resources.institution }}.

Who can access MatchMiner?

We currently maintain three levels of access:,

  • Trial Search is open to all, and does not require a user name or password.
  • Patient-Centric Matching, including molecular results and trial matching, is available to all Oncologists who currently have access to Profile reports via OncoPanel Results Viewer. The approved user list is automatically synced on a nightly basis.
  • Patient identification and notification is only available to approved clinical trial investigators.

Access approval is granted by the DFCI Office of Clinical Research. Visit our Apply for Access page for instructions to apply for any level of access.
