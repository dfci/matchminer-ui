Methodology

ImmunoProfile is a test that quantifies the expression of immune-regulatory proteins within the tumor microenvironment.

Description

ImmunoProfile uses multiplex immunofluorescent staining and imaging. Multiplex immunofluorescent staining is done on FFPE samples. This technology uses multiple colors to identify different cells and proteins at the same time on the same slide. Once multiplex staining is complete, an image of the slide is acquired for analysis. Image analysis software is then used to determine the number and percentage of cells positive for the selected biomarkers.

The current version of ImmunoProfile tests for 6 different proteins/markers on one slide: DAPI, PD-L1 (clone E1L3N), CD8, PD-1, FOXP3 and a tumor marker (Cytokeratin, PAX8, PAX5, SOX10). These proteins have been selected because they are targets for existing immunotherapies and there are publications supporting their use as predictors of response.

Regions/Scores

Intra-Tumor (IT) Intra-tumor is the region of tumor cells beyond the tumor-stroma interface.
Tumor-Stroma Interface (TSI) Tumor-stroma interface is the region within 40 microns on either side of the defined border between tumor and stroma.
Intra-Tumor and Tumor-Stroma Interface (IT + TSI) The intra-tumor region combined with the tumor-stroma interface contains the tumor region plus an additional 40 microns of stroma beyond the defined border between tumor and stroma.
PD-L1: Tumor Proportion Score (TPS) Tumor Proportion Score (TPS) is the percentage of viable tumor cells showing PD-L1 staining relative to all viable tumor cells.
PD-L1: Combined Positive Score (CPS) Combined Positive Score (CPS) is the percentage of PD-L1 staining cells (tumor cells and cells that are non-tumor) relative to all viable tumor cells.
PD-L1: Inflammatory Cell Area Score (IC) Area of PD-L1+ Inflammatory Cells over the area of TSI + IT as a percentage.
PD-L1: Inflammatory Proportion Score (non-tumor cells) Percentage of inflammatory cells (non-tumor cells) showing PD-L1 staining relative to all inflammatory cells.
